About Me

From a very early age I always knew that I wanted to be...a scientist. You thought I was going to say software engineer huh? Don't worry I'm gonna get to that. I'm pretty sure you're a busy person, so to make a long story short, how I ended up wanting to become a software engineer was quite honestly by taking a leap of faith and diving head first into something new. After much deliberation I eventually decided to attend a coding bootcamp; and that's where I had my epiphany. I never actually viewed coding or Computer Science as an 'authentic' field of science until I attended Flatiron School of Software Engineering. While there, I came to realize that coding requires calculations, predictions, experimentation, testing, discovery, and most importantly...curiosity; all the things I enjoyed about being a scientist. Software engineers are scientists! That realization made me want to become a software engineer. I guess in a way I always wanted to be a software engineer and without knowing it.

As far as software I would like to create in the future(besides creating web apps of course),I'm really intersted in getting into mobile developement and possibly build a few useful apps or an annoyingly addictive game. I'm also interested in getting involved with machine learning or even exploring VR. But that'll most likely be a bit further into the future.

If I am fortunate enough to be selected for this internship, I would really like to learn how to communicate effectively in a professional team environment among more experienced developers. I think good communication is one of the most crucial skills a software dev--well actually anyone for that matter--can learn. I'm also interested in learning what I can expect when joining an existing project and how different phases of product developement (from idea to exucution) is accomplished in a professional environment.
